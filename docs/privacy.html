Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

RemoteCode (“we”, “our”, or “the app”) is the iOS and Apple Watch companion for sending remote coding tasks to your IDE. This policy describes how the app handles information.

Summary

RemoteCode is designed so your coding tasks and project data stay in your own cloud storage (iCloud). We do not operate servers that store or process your prompts, code, or project files. The app does not sell your data and does not use it for cross-app tracking.

Information you provide

  • Coding tasks and attachments — Text, voice recordings, photos, and files you attach are written to your iCloud Drive container and read by the RemotePromptCode plugin on your computer.
  • Cloud account — When you connect iCloud, the app uses your device’s signed-in Apple ID. We do not receive your Apple ID password.

Information stored on your device

The app may store connection preferences and local settings (for example, which cloud provider is connected) using standard on-device storage. This data is not transmitted to us.

Device permissions

  • Microphone — Used when you record voice task requests.
  • Speech Recognition — Used to transcribe voice requests on device when enabled.
  • Photo Library — Used when you attach images or files to a task.
  • iCloud — Used to sync RemoteCode folders between your phone and computer.

You can revoke permissions at any time in iPhone Settings.

Third-party services

RemoteCode syncs through Apple iCloud, which is governed by Apple’s Privacy Policy. We do not control Apple’s handling of data in iCloud.

Data we collect

RemoteCode does not collect personal data for its own analytics or advertising. We do not use the app to track you across other companies’ apps or websites.

Children

RemoteCode is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will reflect any changes.
